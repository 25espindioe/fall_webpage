History of Christmas

Earliest Mentions

Christmas came from the second century, where christians celebrated the holiday in order to remember the birth of jesus christ, even though they didn't agree on a set date. The earliest time christmas was marked on the 25th of december is in a roman calendar, known as the chronograph of 354.


image by Gerad van Honthorst

Choice of Date

Many factors allowed christmas to be marked on the 25th. Winter solstice was on that day in the Roman Empire, and most christans lived in Rome. Rome selibrated a Roman festival called Dies Natalis Solis Invicti (birthday of Sol Invictus, the 'invincible sun' and the Christians linked jesus christ to that event, letting christmas be on December 25th.)
